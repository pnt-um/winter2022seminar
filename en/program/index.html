8:45am - 9:15am Digital Transformation in Healthcare from Smart Health to Smart City
Asst. Prof. Sumonta Kasemvilas, Ph.D. (College of Computing, Khon Kaen University, Thailand)
Khon Kaen Smart Living Lab (KKLL) is a digital health service platform in Thailand. It provides testbed, training, operation and service such as a smart dashboard for governments and smart health and smart living portal for citizens. The KKLL collaboration includes academic, government, and private sectors. We conduct a variety of digital health research and innovation projects, including MANEE (preventive healthcare service), Smart ICU, Ambulance Operating Center (AOC), Long Term Care Digital Innovation, Youth Meter, Heath Avatar, Health AI, and Mine Mind. Khon Kaen Smart City is divided into three sections: the medical hub, the transportation hub, and the MICE city. It includes the construction of an excellent medical service center and an accident-emergency center for the medical hub. KKLL works with a variety of partners to develop digital health solutions for Khon Kaen Smart City. The KKLL's future plans include: 1) building a Smart City Intelligent Operation Center (IOC) and data platform, X-Road, to exchange health data in a single standard platform; 2) making this initiative a government policy so that it can be applied to citizens; and 3) promoting the initiative so that people can have inexpensive health sensors and smart technologies to monitor and anticipate health conditions in the long run.
